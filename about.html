About Me

My name is Christian France and I am a second year student at the University of Cincinnati studying Computer Science.

In high school I worked at a bagel shop as a Shift Supervisor. This was a really cool job for me because I was given a lot of responsibilities at a young age. My main takeaways from working there were discipline and dealing with difficult people. The owner of the shop constantly drilled me on everything I did which in turn pushed me towards perfection. There were times where I got frustrated with him, but I'm grateful he taught me so much at such a young age. Being a teenage supervisor meant I had to deal with other workers my age and even older. Leading a team was an eye opening experience at dealing with people and has greatly improved my interaction skills and how to deal with different types of people.

I came to college with a strong interest in Computer Engineering because in high school we worked a lot with electronic hardware and I had built my own PC so it sounded very cool! I had an even stronger interest in programming so it seemed like the perfect major. However, COVID came along and we switched to online classes. I was taking a lot of electrical engineering courses, I had no drive to do anything and I felt stuck. I made the decision to switch to Computer Science to pursue my passion of programming and have never been happier in school!

I am currently looking for a Summer 2021 Co-op and dying to gain some real world experience! I do not have any real world experience and can't wait for the right company to offer me an oppurtunity to learn!
